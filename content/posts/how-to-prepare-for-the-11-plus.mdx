The 11+ exam is one of the most important assessments a child will face in primary school. With the right preparation, it doesn't have to be overwhelming.

## Start early

Ideally, preparation should begin at the start of Year 5. This gives children 12–18 months to build confidence across all four areas: verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English.

## Focus on the fundamentals

Before tackling practice papers, make sure your child has a strong grasp of:

- Times tables up to 12×12
- Spelling rules and common exception words
- Basic grammar and punctuation
- Shape, space and measure

## Use timed practice papers

The 11+ is as much about speed as it is about accuracy. Once your child is comfortable with the content, introduce timed conditions to build exam technique.

## Keep it positive

Anxiety is one of the biggest barriers to performance. Short, regular sessions (30–45 minutes) are far more effective than marathon cramming. Celebrate progress, not just results.
